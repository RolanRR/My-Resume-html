Senior Assembly and Test Technician (N25)

November 1,2019 - November 30,2023

  1. Perform Assembly and Test operations on simple to complex actuator programs.
  2. Troubleshoot test outliers by making adjustments to hardware in order to meet test parameters and initiate possible solutions.
  3. Perform diagnostic works on complex actuator units not conforming to standards and applies appropriate adjustments.
  4. Recommend improvements on Assembly and Test procedures in conjunction with Product Engineering.
  5. Set - up, operate and calibrate functional, automated electronics and mechanical hydraulic test rigs and equipments.
  6. Maintain competency requirement of N24.

Assembly and Test Technician II (N24)

March 16,2017 - October 31,2019

  1. Perform assembly operation on complex actuators and other related hydraulic assemblies following work instructions.
  2. Perform diagnostic works on complex actuator units not conforming to standards and applies appropriate adjustments for rework.
  3. Recommend improvements on Assembly procedures in conjunction with Product Engineering.
  4. Implement technical training activities to newly hired AssemblyTechnician 1 and 2 to cover theoretical concepts, and in the acquisition of assembly skills and techniques.
  5. Perform (in the assembly related activities) in depth analysis, troubleshooting and repair/rework of complex actuator systems in his line of expertise along with PE, TE and QA.
  6. Expert in assembly operation for at least 2 complex actuator from other clusters plus at least a three rating in the related sub-assemblies. This is to be considered as 2nd job (flexibility across clusters).
  7. Maintain certification in the identified certification processes outlined in the education and training requirements.
  8. Implement training activities to new employees on assembly and sub-assembly processes.
  9. Maintain competency requirement of N23.

Assembly and Test Technician I (N23)

December 1,2014 - March 14,2017

  1. Perform assembly and test operations on subassembly components following the work instructions.
  2. Read, interpret assembly drawings, worksheets, part lists, engineering orders, EP’s and EI’s, and other authorized instructions, both written and oral and apply according to standards.
  3. Perform cleaning of assembly and sub-assembly components to high cleanliness levels.
  4. Verify completeness of incoming complex actuator assembly and sub-assembly kits against list.
  5. Visually inspect parts against non-conformities such as dents, scratches, rusts, etc. on actuator parts and subassemblies.
  6. Utilize a variety of mechanical measuring equipment such as torque wrench, dial indicators, calipers and precision micrometers and other appropriate tools.
  7. Perform diagnostic works on units not conforming to standards and apply appropriate adjustments for rework.
  8. Perform hipot, insulation resistance check, coil resistance check on actuators subassembly components.
  9. Recommend improvements on sub-assembly procedures in conjunction with Product Engineering.
  10. Train and guide new employees on sub-assembly processes so that they can perform according to standards.
  11. Identify key physical characteristics of various actuator models and components to ensure product quality and reliability, using appropriate instruments.
  12. Acquire certification in the identified certification processes outlined in the education and training requirements.
  13. Perform generic operations such as; Tail end operations (spre, sern).
